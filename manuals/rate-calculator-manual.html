Performance Rate Advisor User Manual

Getting Started

The Performance Rate Advisor helps performers, musicians, DJs, and entertainers calculate fair compensation rates by analyzing economic data including venue price inflation, supply costs, cost of living increases, and regional market differences.

How to Use

Dashboard Tab

  1. 1.
    Select Your Base Year: Choose the year when you established your current rate. This serves as your baseline for comparisons.
  2. 2.
    Choose Your Region: Select the geographic area where you primarily perform. Regional multipliers account for local market differences.
  3. 3.
    Select Performance Type: Choose the category that best describes your service (Solo Acoustic, DJ, Full Band, etc.).
  4. 4.
    Enter Your Rate: Input the fee you were charging in your selected base year.
  5. 5.
    Toggle Overhead Costs: Enable this if you want to factor in travel, gas, and accommodation expenses for touring.
  6. 6.
    Review Your Rate Card: See recommended rates based on different economic factors:
    • • Cost of Living: Matches general inflation
    • • Market Adjusted: Matches venue menu price increases (recommended)
    • • Supply Match: Matches the increase in venue supply costs
    • • Touring Premium: Accounts for gas and hotel inflation (when overhead is enabled)

Tour Mode Tab

Calculate adjusted rates when performing in different regions. Select your home base and target destination to see the equivalent rate that accounts for regional cost differences.

Negotiate Tab

Generate professional email templates for rate negotiations. The tool provides data-driven talking points and customized correspondence based on your specific situation.

Understanding the Metrics

Venue Menu Prices

Tracks how much venue food and beverage prices have increased based on the Consumer Price Index (CPI). This is typically the most relevant metric for performers.

Venue Supply Costs

Shows the increase in wholesale supply costs using the Producer Price Index (PPI). Venues often cite this when resisting rate increases.

Cost of Living

General inflation rate that affects your personal expenses. This represents the minimum increase needed to maintain purchasing power.

Gas & Rent Prices

Overhead costs that affect touring performers. Gas prices impact travel expenses while rent affects your base operating costs.

Best Practices

  • Use the "Market Adjusted" rate as your primary negotiating point
  • Update your baseline year periodically as you raise rates
  • Use Tour Mode when negotiating rates in new markets
  • Reference the economic data in your negotiations to support your position
  • Copy and customize the generated email templates

Negotiation Strategy

Pro Tip: When venues push back on rate increases, point out that their menu prices have increased by the same percentage you're requesting. This creates a fair and logical argument that's hard to dispute.

The data shows that if venues can raise prices to match inflation, performers should be able to do the same. You're not asking for a premium—you're asking to maintain the same relative value you provided in your base year.
