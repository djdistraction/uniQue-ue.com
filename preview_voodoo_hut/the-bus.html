Origin Story

Before the Venue,
There Was a Bus.

Nine years ago, on a stretch of Kemah waterfront nobody was paying attention to, a converted bus pulled up and didn't leave. That was the beginning.

🚌 You found it.

Where It Started

The Bus Was the Bar

What became The Voodoo Hut started as something that had no business working — a converted school bus parked on the edge of the Kemah waterfront, serving cold drinks to anyone who wandered over. No stage. No screens. No permits for any of what came next.

It worked anyway. People sat outside, music played from a Bluetooth speaker, and something happened that doesn't get engineered — a community formed. The bus wasn't the destination. It was the spark.

The story of The Voodoo Hut is the story of what happens when you build something for the love of it, and the neighborhood decides to show up. Nine years later, we're still that bus. Just bigger. A lot bigger.

From Bus to Broadcast

Year 1

The Bus

Year 2–3

First real stage. First live band. First night where nobody wanted to leave. The idea became a place.
